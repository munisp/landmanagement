# ISO 27001 and Security Governance Policy Pack

## Access Control Policy

Access to the platform shall follow least-privilege and role-based authorization. Administrative, registrar, surveyor, lender, broker, and citizen-access roles must be explicitly approved, periodically reviewed, and revoked when no longer justified. Elevated actions such as suspension, policy changes, privacy-data actions, financial approvals, and security operations must be auditable.

## Incident Response Plan

Security incidents shall be classified by severity, triaged, contained, eradicated, and recovered through documented workflows. Operators must preserve logs, isolate compromised components when necessary, notify stakeholders according to severity, and complete a post-incident review with corrective actions and evidence retention.

## Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

Critical services must have recovery priorities, fallback procedures, restoration order, and owner assignments. Recovery objectives should prioritize identity, registry, payments, workflow orchestration, and audit integrity. Restoration activities must verify application health, data consistency, and rollback safety before resuming full traffic.

## Risk Assessment and Management

Risks shall be logged with impact, likelihood, owner, mitigations, and review cadence. The registry should track technical, operational, compliance, financial, and third-party integration risks. High-impact items require explicit mitigation plans, escalation thresholds, and residual-risk acceptance decisions.

## Data Classification and Handling

Data shall be classified as public, internal, confidential, or restricted. Personal data, financial data, verification records, audit trails, and security evidence require enhanced handling, access control, retention, redaction, and transmission safeguards. Non-production data exports must be minimized and sanitized.

## Change Management

All production changes must be reviewed, tested, traceable, and reversible. Releases should include validation evidence, rollout strategy, rollback conditions, and post-deployment monitoring. Emergency changes require retrospective review and permanent remediation actions.

## Vulnerability Management

Dependencies, infrastructure, and application surfaces shall be reviewed through scheduled scanning, issue triage, remediation deadlines, and retesting. Critical vulnerabilities must receive expedited handling, compensating controls if patching is delayed, and documented closure evidence.

## Vendor Management and Third-Party Risk

External providers must be assessed for security posture, contractual obligations, incident handling, data handling, and continuity commitments. Integration owners must track credentials, quotas, failure modes, and provider-specific rollback or failover actions.

## Security Awareness Training

Personnel must receive onboarding and periodic refreshers covering phishing, credential hygiene, privacy obligations, incident escalation, document handling, and privileged-operation safeguards. Training completion should be recorded and reviewed.

## Compliance Audit Checklist

Audit preparation must confirm policy currency, control evidence, access reviews, incident logs, vulnerability remediation status, privacy operations, backup and recovery procedures, and deployment/change records. Evidence should be organized by control domain and retention requirements.

## Evidence Collection Templates

Each control domain should retain standardized evidence headers including control name, system scope, owner, date range, evidence source, validation method, reviewer, and disposition. Examples include screenshots, logs, exported reports, configuration snapshots, test outputs, and signed approvals.

## Network Security and Cryptography

Network access should be segmented, monitored, and protected by explicit ingress controls, policy enforcement, and secure transport. Cryptographic materials must be inventoried, rotated, access-controlled, and associated with clear owner responsibilities. Secrets should not be embedded in client-delivered assets.

## Monitoring and Logging Procedures

Monitoring must cover application health, security events, workflow failures, queue backlogs, payment exceptions, and policy violations. Logging must support forensic review while minimizing unnecessary exposure of sensitive content. Alert routing and escalation ownership must be defined.

## Maintenance and Review Cadence

This policy pack should be reviewed at least annually and after major architectural, regulatory, or incident-driven changes. Owners must update linked procedures, evidence maps, and training materials whenever policy expectations materially change.
